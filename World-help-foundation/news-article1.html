Reaching New Heights: Education for Refugees

Posted on June 15, 2025 | By World Help Foundation

In the wake of global conflicts, millions of children are displaced from their homes and unable to access quality education. World Help Foundation has been working tirelessly to ensure that displaced children in refugee camps around the world receive the education they deserve, giving them the chance to build a brighter future.

Our Initiative: Bringing Education to Refugee Children

World Help Foundation has launched an innovative education program aimed at bringing schooling to children in the most challenging environments. Through a network of mobile schools, solar-powered classrooms, and remote learning platforms, we are reaching refugee children who would otherwise miss out on education.

Our Approach to Education

By combining technology with traditional classroom structures, our education programs allow children to access curriculum materials, interact with teachers remotely, and participate in life-changing learning experiences.

We have built solar-powered learning centers in refugee camps, where students can access digital lessons, books, and other educational tools. These programs have already helped thousands of children and are expanding rapidly.

What We've Achieved So Far

  • 500+ mobile learning centers deployed across refugee camps.
  • Over 20,000 children enrolled in our education programs.
  • 98% of students reported an improvement in their learning outcomes.
